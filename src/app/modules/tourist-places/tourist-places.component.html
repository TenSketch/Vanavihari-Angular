Gudisa View Point, nestled in the Gudisa Hill range of Andhra Pradesh, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Eastern Ghats. Famous for its sweeping grasslands, lush valleys, dense forests, and mist-covered hills, it is a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

The journey includes a challenging 12 km stretch along the Gudisa Ghat Road, with sharp bends and unpaved paths, leading to open grasslands atop hills over 1,235 meters in altitude. These unique high-altitude ecosystems feature sparse, stunted vegetation, semi-evergreen forests, and rich biodiversity, including several Rare, Endangered, and Threatened (RET) speciesâ€”three of which are found only here. Streams meandering through rocky terrains add to the charm, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Located within the Maredumilli Range of the Rampachodavaram Division Reserve Forests, Gudisa Hills is also home to the Konda Reddy tribe. Visitors can explore preserved tribal arts, crafts, and traditional knowledge, along with guided tours covering trekking trails, birdwatching spots (including the rare Green Munia), and nature education programs.

Tourism here is regulated to protect the fragile ecosystemâ€”plastic is banned, overnight stays are prohibited, and sustainable practices are encouraged to ensure its beauty endures for generations.
